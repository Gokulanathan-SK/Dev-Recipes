The Art of Dosa Making

Description

Dosa is a lacey thin crepe made with fermented rice and lentil batter. Itâ€™s a giant crispy crepe and is served for breakfast. The most popular type of dosa is masala dosa, a savory crepe filled with spicy potato mash made with potatoes, spices, onions, and curry leaves. Dosa is served with vegetable sambar and coconut chutney. Dosa is a delicious creation.

Ingredients

  • Rice: 2 cups (preferably parboiled rice or dosa rice)
  • Urad Dal (Split Black Gram): 1/2 cup
  • Fenugreek Seeds: 1/2 teaspoon (optional, helps in fermentation and adds flavor)
  • Water: As needed for soaking and grinding
  • Salt: To taste
  • Oil or Ghee: For greasing the pan (you can use vegetable oil, sesame oil, or clarified butter)

Step by Step Process

  1. Soaking: Rinse the rice and urad dal separately in water. Soak the rice and fenugreek seeds together in water for about 4-6 hours. Soak the urad dal (and chana dal if using) separately for about 4-6 hours. If using poha, soak it in water for about 30 minutes before grinding.
  2. Grinding: Drain the soaked rice and dal. Grind the urad dal with a little water to a smooth and fluffy batter. Transfer it to a large bowl. Grind the rice and fenugreek seeds (and poha if using) with a little water to a smooth batter. Combine it with the urad dal batter. Mix both batters thoroughly and add salt. The consistency should be slightly thick but pourable
  3. Fermentation: Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight until it doubles in volume and becomes airy.
  4. Making Dosa: Heat a non-stick pan or a cast-iron tawa over medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil or ghee. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the pan and spread it out in a circular motion to form a thin layer. Drizzle a few drops of oil or ghee around the edges and on top. Cook until the edges start to lift and the bottom turns golden brown. Flip if desired (though traditional dosas are usually not flipped). Remove the dosa from the pan and serve hot with chutney, sambar, or any preferred accompaniments.
