The Art of Sambar Making

Description

Sambar is a spicy lentil-based vegetable stew. The stew is made with lentils, vegetables such as tomatoes, okra, shallots, radishes, drumsticks and tamarind paste, herbs, and sambar powder. Sambar powder, also known as huli pudi is made by roasting and grinding chana dal, urad dal, mustard seeds, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, asafoetida cloves, and whole red chilies. Sambar is a versatile dish and accompanies everything from dosas to rice. Sambar is spicy and tangy.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup tooar dal (split pigeon peas)
  • Water for soaking and cooking
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (or ghee)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • ¾ cup chopped vegetables (such as drumstick, carrots, eggplant, pumpkin, or green beans) - optional
  • 1 teaspoon sambar powder (or to taste)
  • 1 tamarind pulp extract (small lemon-sized ball)
  • 1 teaspoon jaggery (or brown sugar)
  • Salt to taste
  • ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • ¼ teaspoon red chili powder (optional)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 1 dry red chili (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon chopped coriander leaves (for garnish)

Step by Step Process

  1. Cook the Dal: Rinse the toor dal in water until the water runs clear. Soak the dal in enough water for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight. In a pot, add the soaked dal and enough fresh water to cover it by about 2 inches. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the dal is tender and cooked through, about 20-25 minutes. You can also pressure cook the dal for faster cooking (around 5 whistles). Once cooked, mash the dal slightly with a potato masher or whisk.
  2. Prepare the Vegetables (Optional): While the dal simmers, you can prepare your vegetables. Wash and chop your chosen vegetables into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Make the Sambar Base: Heat oil in a separate pan or pot. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add curry leaves, dry red chili (if using), and hing (asafoetida) for a few seconds until fragrant. Add chopped onions and saute until translucent. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until softened and mushy. Add the chopped vegetables (if using) and cook for a few minutes until slightly tender.
  4. Add Spices and Flavorings: Add sambar powder, turmeric powder, and red chili powder (if using) to the pan. Stir and cook for a minute, releasing the aroma of the spices.
  5. Simmer with Dal and Tamarind: Pour the cooked and mashed dal into the pan with the vegetable and spice mix. Add tamarind pulp extract and jaggery (or brown sugar). Season with salt to taste. Add enough water to achieve your desired consistency for the sambar. Bring the sambar to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  6. Garnish and Serve: Turn off the heat and stir in chopped coriander leaves for a fresh touch. Serve hot with rice, idli, or dosa.
