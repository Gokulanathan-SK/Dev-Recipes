The Art of Idli Making

Description

Idlis are steamed rice cakes made from fermented rice and lentil batter. The batter is steamed into circular molds. The result is fluffy moon-like savory rice cakes. It is served with vegetable sambar and various chutneys. It can be eaten by itself. Other types of idlis include vegetable idlis as carrots and green beans are added to the rice batter. Rava idli is made from semolina, yogurt, and spices. Idlis are light, fluffy, and flavorful.

Ingredients

  • Rice - 2 cups (preferably idli rice)
  • Urad Dal (split black gram) - 1 cup
  • Fenugreek Seeds (Methi) - 1 teaspoon
  • Water: As needed for soaking and grinding
  • Salt: To taste
  • Oil - for greasing the idli molds

Step by Step Process

  1. Soak the Ingredients: Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 4-6 hours. Wash the urad dal and fenugreek seeds together and soak them in water for about 4-6 hours.
  2. Grinding: Drain the soaked rice and urad dal. Grind the urad dal and fenugreek seeds (if using) first with a little water to a smooth and fluffy batter. You can use a blender or a wet grinder for this.Grind the soaked rice separately to a slightly coarse batter. Combine the rice and urad dal batters in a large bowl.
  3. Fermentation: Add salt to the combined batter and mix well. Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours, or until doubled in size. The ideal temperature for fermentation is around 75°F (24°C). If your kitchen is cooler, you can use a warm oven on the "proof" setting or place the bowl in a pot of warm water.
  4. Steam the idlis: Grease the idli molds with oil. Pour the fermented batter into the molds, filling them about ¾ full. In a steamer, bring water to a boil. Place the idli molds in the steamer and steam for 10-12 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into an idli comes out clean.
  5. Serve: Let the idlis cool slightly in the molds before removing them with a spoon. Serve idlis hot with sambar and coconut chutney.
