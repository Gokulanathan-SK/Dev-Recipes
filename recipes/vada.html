The Art of Vada Making

Description

Vada is a popular Indian street food. Vada is a donut-shaped fritter made from a batter of ground lentils, spices, and curry leaves. Vada is served with sambar or rasam and also eaten with mint chutney. In North India, vada is known as bhalla. In dahi bhalla, vada is dunked into whisked creamy yogurt and topped with mint and tamarind chutneys. Vada is everyone's favorite comfort food.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup urad dal (whole black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon chopped green chili (optional)
  • A few curry leaves (chopped, optional)
  • 1-2 tablespoons rice flour (optional)
  • 1 pinch hing (asafoetida, optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 3-4 tablespoons water (or as needed)
  • Oil for deep frying

Step by Step Process

  1. Soak the urad dal: Rinse the urad dal in water a couple of times until the water runs clear. Soak the urad dal in at least 2-3 cups of water for 4-5 hours, or up overnight.
  2. Grind the batter: Drain the soaked urad dal completely. Add the urad dal to a blender or grinder jar. Grind the urad dal with minimal water (1-2 tablespoons at a time) to a smooth and fluffy batter. The consistency should be thicker than pancake batter but still pourable. Tip: If your batter seems too thin, it might not hold its shape well while frying. You can add a tablespoon or two of rice flour to thicken it up.
  3. Season and ferment (optional): Add salt, chopped green chili (if using), curry leaves (if using), and hing (if using) to the batter and mix well. You can skip this fermentation step for quicker vadas. However, fermenting for 30 minutes to an hour can help make the vadas lighter and crispier. To ferment, cover the batter and let it sit in a warm place for about 30 minutes to an hour.
  4. Fry the vadas: Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai over medium heat. Ideally, the oil temperature should be around 350°F (175°C). To check the oil temperature, you can drop a tiny bit of batter into the oil. If it sizzles and floats to the surface immediately, the oil is hot enough. Wet your hands with water to prevent the batter from sticking. Take a small portion of batter (about a lemon-sized ball) and shape it into a donut shape with a hole in the center. You can use a mold or your thumb to make the hole. Gently slide the vada into the hot oil. Don't overcrowd the pan. Fry the vadas for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy. Once fried, remove the vadas from the oil with a slotted spoon and drain them on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  5. Serve: Serve vadas hot with sambar and coconut chutney for a delicious South Indian breakfast or snack.
